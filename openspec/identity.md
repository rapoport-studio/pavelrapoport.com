# Pavel Rapoport — Identity

> What I feel, what I say, and what I do — must be the same thing.

---

## Story

I was 10 when I wrote my first Arkanoid in Basic at a
pioneer house in Chișinău. 35 years, three countries,
four languages later — I'm still building.

I started as a translator — turning business problems
into interfaces. Buttons, forms, screens. Then I became
a system builder — infrastructure that entire teams
depended on: design systems, monorepos, pipelines. Five
design systems from scratch, a thousand components.
One of the companies where I was an early engineer was
acquired for $490M.

I spent years in corporations. I learned a lot — security,
scale, big data, media management. But I also learned what
kills me: environments where nobody takes responsibility
for anything new. Where people talk more than they build.
Where a good idea gets rejected not because it's wrong,
but because change is uncomfortable. I kept pushing —
new technologies, new approaches, AI pipelines. Most of
it was ignored. Eventually, the corporation and I parted
ways. They didn't need someone who can't stop building.
I didn't need permission to build.

In between — divorce, a car accident, bankruptcy, two
court cases, a war. And I'm still here. Still building.

But the most important thing I built in the last year
isn't code. I learned to separate what actually happened
from the story I told myself about it. Sounds simple.
In practice, it changes everything: how I evaluate
projects, how I talk to clients, how I make decisions.

Now I'm rebuilding from scratch. New positioning, new
business, possibly a new country. Twenty years of
experience in Israeli startups, cybersecurity, and
creative tech — packed into a platform that works the
way I think.

## Philosophy

**Congruence.** One word that holds everything together.
What I feel, what I say, and what I do — must match.
This applies to code: if the architecture says one thing
and the implementation does another — the system breaks.
If I promise a client one thing and build another — the
relationship breaks. Congruence is an engineering principle
applied to life.

**Nothing ships without a spec.** I don't open the IDE
until there's an answer to "what exactly are we building
and why." This isn't bureaucracy — it's respect for other
people's time and money.

**Results, not technology.** I don't sell React or Next.js.
I sell: your team stops waiting 15 minutes per build.
Designers and developers speak the same language. An idea
becomes a product — not an endless backlog.

**Honesty over comfort.** If the project doesn't make
sense — I'll say it. If the problem can be solved simpler
— I'll suggest simpler. I don't inflate scope for hours.
I build what's needed and stop.

**Three eras.** Translator → System Builder → Entity Designer.
First I built interfaces for people. Then systems for teams.
Now — architecture for a new kind of entity: AI agents,
automations, workflows that run without a human in the loop.
The world is changing. Interfaces too.

**The corporation lesson.** I spent years in environments
that reward maintenance over innovation. I don't blame
them — that's their survival strategy. But I know now:
I need to be where building is the point, not the exception.
This platform is that place.

**Impulse is a feature, not a bug.** I feel technology
before I understand it. I act before the spreadsheet is
ready. For years I thought this was a flaw. Now I know:
in a world that moves this fast, the ability to feel
direction and move immediately — that's the advantage.
The platform gives structure to the impulse. OpenSpec is
the rail. The energy is mine.

## Values

- **Build, don't talk.** Shipping is the proof.
- **Clarity over cleverness.** Simple systems that work
  beat complex systems that impress.
- **Own the mistake.** When something breaks — say it,
  fix it, move on. No hiding.
- **Learn every day.** This project is a learning machine.
  Every feature teaches something new. Discipline and joy
  go together.
- **Delegate to grow.** Everything that can be automated,
  will be. Everything that can be delegated, will be.
  Not to avoid work — to focus on what only I can do.
- **Respect the craft.** Twenty years of other people's
  projects taught me: every codebase is someone's dream.
  Treat it that way.

## Who I am in this AI world

The world is splitting into two: people who use AI as
a tool, and people who design systems where AI is a
participant. I'm in the second group.

I don't prompt ChatGPT to write emails. I build
architectures where AI agents have roles, permissions,
tasks, and accountability — just like humans. The domain
map doesn't care if the entity is a person or a bot.
The spec doesn't care who implements it.

This is what I mean by Entity Designer. The new interface
isn't a screen — it's a system of entities that includes
humans, AI, robots, and automations, all connected through
a domain architecture.

My job: design that architecture. Connect the pieces.
Make sure it's secure, scalable, and congruent.
